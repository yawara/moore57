Why participate?

Whether a Moore graph of degree 57 and diameter 2 exists is a long-standing open problem in algebraic combinatorics. Each entry on the tracker is a concrete piece of that puzzle: a candidate group / order that, if ruled out, narrows the structure of any hypothetical Aut(Γ) — or eventually shows Aut(Γ) must be trivial.

The natural-language proofs already exist (Aschbacher 1971, Makhnev– Paduchikh 2001, Macaj–Siran 2010). The challenge — and the opportunity — is to turn them into machine-checked Lean 4 / Mathlib theorems. Three orders (22, 38, 110) are already unconditionally formalized; 49 finite groups across 10 candidate orders remain open.

Ways to help

SkillWhat to do
Lean 4 / Mathlib Pick an open order (see below) and Lean-ify the corresponding Macaj–Siran row. Most surviving cases follow the structure of our existing Order22OnMoore57/ and C38OnMoore57/ modules.
Finite-group theory Help refine the SmallGroup tables on the per-order pages (GAP IDs, conjugacy of semidirect products, action lattice on Sylow subgroups). Especially needed for orders 54, 135, 147, 375.
Paper-side review Walk through Macaj–Siran Lemma 12 / 13 / 15 and identify gaps, alternative routes, or computational checks worth formalising. Lemma 15 in particular has several "starred" cases that the paper notes are impossible but does not fully derive.
GAP / SageMath Build supporting computations (orbit structures, character tables of candidate subgroups, equitable partitions) that inform or replace ad-hoc case analysis in Lean.
Math review Cross-check the Lean proofs in Moore57/ against the natural-language sources. Spot-check Phase 1–7 of the D₁₉ proof, or the Hoffman–Singleton classification in HoffmanSingleton.lean.
Writing / outreach Blog notes (see blogs/), arxiv preprints, or talks. We are particularly interested in producing a self-contained writeup of the D₁₉ proof for arXiv submission.

Pick a target

All 10 open orders are listed on the top page. A rough "approachability" ranking based on the existing infrastructure:

14 2 · 7 0 / 2 open closest analogue of order 22 50 2 · 5² 0 / 5 open order-25 Sylow + involution 35 5 · 7 0 / 1 open single group, Lemma 15 starred row 39 3 · 13 0 / 2 open Lemma 12 row for p = 13 54 2 · 3³ 0 / 15 open 15 groups but Theorem 4 bound helps 375 3 · 5³ 0 / 4 open hardest; GAP-aided in the paper

"Open" here means no Lean proof of no_OrderN_acts_on_Moore57 exists yet. The remaining four open orders (135, 147, 171, 275) are also fair game.

Getting started (Lean)

Prerequisites: a working Lean 4 / Mathlib setup (Lean 4.x + elan). Then: 

git clone https://github.com/yawara/moore57.git
cd moore57
lake build

Build is large (≈ 4000 jobs) and takes 30–60 minutes from scratch. Subsequent rebuilds are incremental. Key entry points:

  • Moore57/Phases/FinalAssembly.lean — the main no_D19_acts_on_Moore57_unconditional theorem.
  • Moore57/Order22OnMoore57/NoGo.lean — template for an "exclude an order N" proof.
  • Moore57/AxiomsCheck.lean — the axiom allowlist; any new theorem you formalize should pass this check.

CI will reject any sorry or admit in proof position. The axiom allowlist is enforced at build time.

Communication

  • GitHub Issues — github.com/yawara/moore57/issues. Open an issue to coordinate on a target before starting work.
  • Pull Requests — small, focused PRs are easier to review than long ones. The CI runs lake build + the axiom check.
  • Blog notes — blogs/YYYYMMDD.md records ongoing thinking. Contributors are encouraged to add entries describing the route they took.

Recognition
