Source

Macaj–Siran 2010, Theorem 7 (even case). The unique candidate of the form 11 · 5 · 2. It is also the largest possible value of |G| in the even case (Corollary 3: |G| ≤ 110).

Group classification (SmallGroup(110, k))

GAP's SmallGroup library lists six entries for order 110; the four above are the canonical metabelian shapes that survive the standard Sylow analysis (n₁₁ = 1, n₅ ∈ {1, 11}).

Proof: reduction to order 22

For |G| = 110 = 2 · 5 · 11:

  1. Sylow's third theorem: n₁₁ ∣ 10 and n₁₁ ≡ 1 (mod 11), so n₁₁ = 1; the unique Sylow-11 subgroup P₁₁ ≅ ℤ₁₁ is normal.
  2. Cauchy's theorem applied to |G| = 110: there exists an involution τ ∈ G.
  3. H := ⟨P₁₁, τ⟩ has order 22 and is a subgroup of G ≤ Aut(Γ).
  4. By no_Order22_group_acts_on_Moore57, no such H exists. Contradiction.

The Macaj–Siran paper itself already excluded order 110 by Lemma 15 (no automorphism of order 110); the Lean proof uses the cleaner "subgroup reduction" route via the unconditional order-22 theorem.
