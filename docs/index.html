Whether a Moore graph Γ of degree 57 and diameter 2 exists is a long-standing open question. If it does, its automorphism group G = Aut(Γ) has a finite order, which by results of Aschbacher, Higman, Makhnev–Paduchikh, and Macaj–Siran satisfies very strong restrictions. This page tracks which of the surviving candidate orders have been ruled out (in Lean 4 / Mathlib) and which remain open.

Want to help close one of these?

This is an open formalization project. 49 finite groups across 10 candidate orders are still open. Each one is a concrete target with a published proof outline in Macaj–Siran (2010), Lemma 12–15. Lean 4 / Mathlib contributors, finite-group specialists, paper-side reviewers, and GAP-aided computations are all welcome.

What "candidate order" means

Every actual |G| divides one of the 13 maxima above. So all candidate orders are precisely the union of divisors of these 13 numbers (roughly 35 distinct values once 1 is included). The per-order pages enumerate them along with their group classifications.
